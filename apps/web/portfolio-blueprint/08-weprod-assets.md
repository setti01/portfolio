# About WeProd

WeProd Maroc is one of the leading communication and event agencies in Tangier, specializing in both event production and 360° communication services. The agency combines creativity, marketing, event management and digital communication to help brands create memorable experiences and strengthen their visibility.

During my time at WeProd, the company had a multidisciplinary team of around 15 professionals, including graphic designers, community managers, videographers, 3D designers, event specialists, HR and administrative staff. Working in this environment gave me my first real experience inside a creative agency.

The agency offers a complete range of services including:

- Event organization
- Brand communication
- Social media management
- Graphic design
- Print production
- Website creation
- SEO
- Advertising
- Event equipment rental
- Artistic booking
- Sponsorship and event branding

WeProd has built a strong reputation in Tangier thanks to the quality of its work, its creativity and its ability to manage large-scale communication and event projects for hotels, restaurants, beauty brands and corporate clients.

# How I Joined WeProd

Before joining WeProd, I was working as a freelance graphic designer. Although I had several clients, freelancing alone wasn't providing enough financial stability, and I wanted to gain experience inside a professional creative agency.

Months before applying, one of my friends who worked at WeProd as a Community Manager often talked about the agency. The name stayed in my mind.

At the beginning of summer 2024, the same friend sent me a contact and told me that WeProd was looking for a Graphic Designer. I immediately applied and waited for a response.

Several days passed without any news. I eventually travelled to Fez to visit my family and completely forgot about the application.

One morning, I woke up to several missed calls from an unknown number. I called back but nobody answered. A few minutes later they called again.

The first sentence I heard was:

> "Bilal, I think you don't really want this job!" 😂

We both laughed, scheduled the interview, and a few days later I attended my very first job interview.

The recruitment process included interviews with the Community Manager, the HR department and finally the agency's founder.

I successfully passed every interview and, to my surprise, I started working the very same day.

It was my first professional experience inside a creative agency.

# First Day

My first day was much less stressful than I expected.

Oumaima, the Community Manager, made a real effort to help me integrate into the team. Instead of immediately assigning work, she organized a few casual games of Connect Four with different members of the communication team to help break the ice.

That simple gesture immediately made me feel welcome.

Another pleasant surprise was discovering how well everything had been organized by the previous graphic designer. Project files, templates and resources were properly classified, making the transition much easier and allowing me to become productive very quickly.

# Daily Workflow

A typical day started at 9:00 AM after a coffee and a couple of cigarettes on the balcony before opening my computer.

The first task was always checking whether the Community Managers had assigned new work through the team's communication group. If no new requests had arrived, I continued unfinished projects from the previous day. Whenever there was free time, I used the agency's computer to improve my own skills and work on personal projects, since my laptop at home wasn't powerful enough.

Every day involved switching between different clients and completely different types of work. In the same morning I could design a thumbnail for a reel, create Instagram stories for a hotel, retouch photographs for the agency's social media or prepare files for printing.

After the lunch break, work continued until 6:00 PM, following the agency's production schedule and client deadlines.

# Featured Project — Traditional Women's Event

The project I am most proud of during my time at WeProd was a large traditional women-only event organized by the agency.

Although I had recently joined the company, I wasn't limited to executing design requests. During one of the project meetings, the agency's founder asked the team to share ideas for the event's marketing strategy.

I actively participated in the discussion and proposed several marketing concepts, including content ideas for social media and a ticketing strategy called the "Blind Ticket".

The idea was simple: instead of revealing the guest artist immediately, attendees could purchase tickets before the lineup was announced. This created curiosity, encouraged early ticket sales and generated conversations around the event.

Seeing one of my ideas become part of the campaign gave me confidence that I could contribute beyond graphic design.

Once the strategy was approved, I became responsible for producing many of the campaign's visual assets.

# Biggest Lesson

One sentence completely changed the way I approach design.

One of my colleagues introduced me to the KISS principle:

> Keep It Stupid Simple.

At first, I didn't understand what it meant.

Over time, I realized that the best designs are often the simplest ones.

Instead of adding unnecessary effects or trying to impress with complexity, I learned to focus on clarity, communication and purpose.

This principle continues to influence the way I design today.

# Biggest Challenges

## Flamant Rose

The biggest creative challenge was working on Flamant Rose.

The communication plan required two social media posts and five Instagram stories every week.

The difficulty wasn't the workload.

It was finding fresh ideas for a hotel that always had the same rooms, the same restaurant and the same spaces.

This project taught me that creativity isn't about making something new every time. It's about presenting familiar things from a different perspective.

# Why I Left

Although I learned a lot at WeProd, I eventually decided to move on.

The experience gave me confidence, discipline and valuable agency experience, but I wanted better career opportunities, continue growing professionally and work in an environment where my skills and contributions would be more highly valued.

Looking back, joining WeProd was one of the best decisions I made early in my career.

# Reflection

WeProd was my first experience inside a professional creative agency.

When I joined, I mainly saw myself as a graphic designer.

When I left, I had a much broader understanding of branding, communication, print production, event marketing and teamwork.

More importantly, I discovered that I enjoy solving problems, contributing ideas and working on complete communication projects—not just designing visuals.

This experience shaped the way I work today and confirmed that I can quickly adapt to new environments, industries and creative challenges.
