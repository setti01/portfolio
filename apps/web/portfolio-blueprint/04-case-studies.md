# Bilal ESSATTE — Case Study Blueprint

> Version 1.0

---

# Philosophy

A case study is not a gallery.

It is a story.

The goal is not to show beautiful images.

The goal is to prove how I think, how I solve problems, and how I create value.

Every case study should answer one question:

"Why should someone trust Bilal with their next project?"

---

# Every Case Study Must Answer

Who was the client?

What problem existed?

Why was it a problem?

What was my role?

What decisions did I make?

Why did I make those decisions?

What changed after the project?

What did I learn?

# 1. Hero

Large project image.

Project name.

Industry.

Client.

Role.

Year.

Duration.

Tools used.

CTA

Visit Website (if available)

---

# 2. Project Overview

One paragraph.

Explain:

Who is the client?

What do they do?

Why did they contact me?

What was the objective?

Maximum:

150 words.

---

# 3. The Challenge

Explain the real problem.

Not:

"The client wanted a website."

Instead:

"The previous website made it difficult for customers to find information and did not reflect the premium quality of the business."

The challenge should feel real.

---

# 4. Discovery & Research

Explain:

How I understood the problem.

Questions I asked.

Competitor analysis.

Audience.

Brand positioning.

Research findings.

Even small projects deserve some explanation.

---

# 5. Strategy

Why this direction?

Explain decisions.

Color choices.

Typography.

Layout.

User journey.

Brand personality.

Business objectives.

This is where agencies evaluate thinking.

---

# 6. Design Process

Show sketches.

Wireframes.

Iterations.

Rejected ideas.

Moodboards.

Before / After.

Explain evolution.

Not perfection.

---

# 7. Final Solution

Gallery.

Desktop.

Tablet.

Mobile.

Brand assets.

Packaging.

Menus.

Social media.

Photography.

Anything relevant.

Large images.

Minimal text.

---

# 8. Results

Always include measurable outcomes if possible.

Examples

Increase in engagement.

More bookings.

Improved readability.

Better customer experience.

Client satisfaction.

Business growth.

Even qualitative results matter.

---

# 9. Lessons Learned

One of the most important sections.

Explain:

What challenged me?

What would I improve today?

What did this project teach me?

Recruiters love reflection.

---

# 10. Next Project

Keep visitors exploring.

Previous / Next navigation.

Related work.

# Writing Style

Explain.

Don't impress.

Use simple language.

Avoid buzzwords.

Show confidence through clarity.

---

# Images

Large.

High quality.

Real work first.

Mockups second.

Avoid decorative mockups.

Show the actual design.

---

# Before / After

Whenever possible include:

Original

↓

Problems

↓

New Solution

This instantly demonstrates value.

---

# Metrics

Good metrics:

Bookings

Sales

Traffic

Engagement

Reach

Orders

Conversion

Customer feedback

Bad metrics:

Hours worked

Number of layers

Number of revisions

---

# AI

Mention AI only when it genuinely improved the workflow.

Explain:

Why?

How?

What remained human?

Never pretend AI created the project.

---

# What Recruiters Care About

Not:

How pretty it is.

Instead:

Can Bilal explain decisions?

Can Bilal work in a team?

Can Bilal solve problems?

Would Bilal fit into our agency?

---

# Every Section Should End With

"So what?"

If the section doesn't answer that question,

rewrite it.

---

# Questions Every Case Study Must Answer

Why was this project necessary?

What constraints existed?

What decisions mattered most?

What trade-offs were made?

What impact did the work have?

What would I improve today?

---

# Common Mistakes

Too many mockups.

No context.

No explanation.

No process.

No business goals.

Too much text.

Too many animations.

No measurable results.

No reflection.

---

# Quality Checklist

Before publishing ask:

Would this impress a Creative Director?

Would this help a recruiter trust me?

Could another designer learn something?

Does every image have a purpose?

Does every paragraph add value?

If not,

keep improving.
